Jiewen Lai received his Ph.D. degree in Mechanical Engineering from The Hong Kong Polytechnic University in 2022. He completed his one-year postdoctoral training at CUHK in 2023, after which he joined the Department of Electronic Engineering at CUHK as a Research Assistant Professor. 

In 2026, he served as a Visiting Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Stanford University. He is currently a Research Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronic Engineering at CUHK. He has authored or co‑authored more than 30 publications, including articles in Nature Reviews Electrical Engineering, The International Journal of Robotics Research, multiple IEEE Transactions journals, book chapters, and international conference proceedings. His research interests include soft robotics, bioinspired robots, and intelligent medical mechatronics for sensing, actuation, and motion control.

Dr. Lai was a recipient of the SYP Award of the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society (2025), the T.J. Tarn Best Paper Award at IEEE ROBIO (2025), the IdeaBooster Fund Award (2022), the Dr. Barbara Kwok Young Postdoctoral Researcher Award (2023), and the Best Paper Finalist Award at IEEE ROBIO (2019). He also serves as an Associate Editor for ICRA (2024, 2025, 2026) and as a Member of the Early Career Editorial Board for Biomimetic Intelligence and Robotics. He is a Member of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS) and a Senior Member of the Chinese Mechanical Engineering Society (CMES).
